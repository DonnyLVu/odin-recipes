Ceviche

Description

Ceviche is a traditional dish from Latin America, particularly popular in countries like Peru, Ecuador, and Mexico. It is typically made by marinating raw fish or shellfish in citrus juices, such as lime or lemon, along with herbs and spices. The acid in the citrus juices "cooks" the fish, giving it a firm texture. Common ingredients in ceviche include fish, cilantro, onions, and chili peppers. It is often served as an appetizer or as a main dish with tortilla chips or tostadas.
